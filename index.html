Predict → observe → explain

Commit a guess before you look. There is no grade — the point is to notice the gap.

Where is the walker most likely to be found, at full coherence?

Near the edges. At γ = 1 the distribution has two peaks close to the ballistic front ±N/√2 and a suppressed centre — the walker most likely ends up far from where it started. This is the headline behaviour; do not bury it.

What happens to the pattern as the paths become distinguishable (coherence removed)?

It collapses to the classical bell curve. Full coherence → the edge-enhanced quantum walk; zero coherence → the binomial Galton board. Because γ is real here, the fringes wash out without sliding — losing coherence, not adding a phase. (A real subtlety lives in the middle; see the advanced layer.)

If you could mark which path was taken, what happens to the fringes?

They weaken and vanish as the marking becomes complete. Marking correlates the path with another degree of freedom; the more distinguishable the paths, the less path-coherence is available to interfere. Note the honest wording: “more distinguishable → weaker fringes,” not “which-path information is decoherence.” See limits of the analogy.

For one balanced interferometer the output intensities are

I± = ½ (1 ± γ cos φ)

At γ = 1 this is the textbook ½(1 ± cos φ): perfect fringes. As γ → 0 the cos term is suppressed and both ports sit at ½ — no fringes. The first equation we show explains a result you have already seen.

What this does — and does not — show

It shows that the same lattice produces a classical bell curve or an edge-enhanced quantum walk depending on one thing: whether the alternative paths stay coherent. The read-out (clicks vs intensity) is just rendering.

What destroys the fringes is incoherence, not “classicality.” A lone photon still interferes with itself — that is the coherent-wave vs incoherent-light contrast, carried by the two right-hand cells, not parked in a footnote.

The quantum-to-classical reading is qualified. “Full coherence = quantum walk; zero coherence = Galton board” holds for the specific per-layer dephasing channel used here (Appendix A): coherence is removed in the directed-edge basis after every layer. Dephasing only at the final read-out, or in a different basis, need not give the same limit.

γ is kept real in v0. The slider washes fringes out without shifting them, deliberately separating loss of coherence from an added phase. Complex γ (magnitude → visibility, phase → shift) is a documented future extension, not a hidden simplification.

For the undergraduate layer

Amplitudes, the unitary, three coherence notions, and the non-monotonic crossover

The unitary, once

The wave and quantum cells share one unitary U = S·Pφ·H per layer (Hadamard coin H, per-cell phase Pφ, then the shift S), followed by the dephasing channel Dγ(ρ) = γρ + (1−γ)·diag(ρ) in the directed-edge (position, coin) basis. For every output j in the coherent limit, Ij ≡ |ψj|² — a software invariant checked by the suite, not a sentence.

Three notions behind one slider

  • Mutual coherence between paths — what the off-diagonals of ρ carry.
  • Which-path distinguishability in an auxiliary degree of freedom.
  • Dephasing noise from uncontrolled phase fluctuations.

All three reduce interference, but they are not operationally identical in a real experiment. v0 exposes one real-γ slider; separating reversible marker entanglement from irreversible ensemble phase noise is deferred.

The non-monotonic crossover (the honest middle)

Coming down from γ = 1, a little dephasing first flattens the horn toward a broad, near-uniform profile before strong dephasing gives the diffusive Gaussian: horn → flattened → binomial, not a smooth morph. The standard deviation σ is a poor way to see this (it falls monotonically); the right diagnostic is the peak height, plotted below — it dips to a minimum at intermediate γ.

Conventions, pinned

Coin: Hadamard. Initial coin state: (|↑⟩ + i|↓⟩)/√2. We verified numerically that this gives a mirror-symmetric P(x) — and, in fact, that the ∓i sign gives the identical distribution (the states are complex conjugates and the evolution is real), so the literature’s sign ambiguity is invisible to the read-out in v0. Single-port input |↑⟩ instead reproduces the drifting Hadamard profile — offered as a contrast, never the default.
